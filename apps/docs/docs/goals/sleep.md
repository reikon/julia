# Peptides for sleep

:::warning Educational use only
This page is informational and is **not medical advice**. It does not recommend any peptide
and does not provide dosing or protocols. Sleep-related peptide claims are largely mechanistic
or anecdotal. Consult a qualified clinician before making any decision.
:::

Sleep covers sleep quality, circadian regulation, and rest-related recovery. Direct human
evidence for peptides in this area is sparse; many claims are downstream of growth-hormone-axis
effects or pineal and melatonin mechanisms. The links below show the strongest applicable
evidence tier.

## Relevant peptides

- [Epitalon](../peptides/epitalon.md) (Preclinical, Mechanistic): proposed pineal and melatonin effects. Not approved.
- [Ipamorelin](../peptides/ipamorelin.md) (Anecdotal, Mechanistic): GH secretagogue; sleep claims are self-reported. Not approved.
- [CJC-1295](../peptides/cjc-1295.md) (Anecdotal, Mechanistic): GHRH analog; sleep claims are self-reported. Not approved.

See [how evidence tiers work](../how-it-works.md) to interpret these labels.
