# Peptides for fat loss

:::warning Educational use only
This page is informational and is **not medical advice**. It does not recommend any peptide
and does not provide dosing or protocols. Some entries are approved prescription medicines
that must be used only under medical supervision; others have only preclinical or anecdotal
support. Consult a qualified clinician before making any decision.
:::

Fat loss and metabolic health is the category with the strongest human clinical evidence in
this reference, because it includes approved GLP-1-class prescription drugs. It also includes
research peptides with much weaker support. The links below show the strongest applicable
evidence tier and the regulatory status.

## Relevant peptides

- [Semaglutide](../peptides/semaglutide.md) (Clinical): approved GLP-1 receptor agonist for weight management and diabetes. Prescription only.
- [Tirzepatide](../peptides/tirzepatide.md) (Clinical): approved dual GIP/GLP-1 receptor agonist. Prescription only.
- [MOTS-c](../peptides/mots-c.md) (Preclinical, Mechanistic): mitochondrial-derived peptide studied for metabolism. Not approved.
- [CJC-1295](../peptides/cjc-1295.md) (Anecdotal, Mechanistic): GHRH analog; body-composition claims are self-reported. Not approved.
- [Ipamorelin](../peptides/ipamorelin.md) (Anecdotal, Mechanistic): GH secretagogue; body-composition claims are self-reported. Not approved.

See [how evidence tiers work](../how-it-works.md) to interpret these labels.
