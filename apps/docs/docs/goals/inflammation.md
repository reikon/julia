# Peptides for inflammation

:::warning Educational use only
This page is informational and is **not medical advice**. It does not recommend any peptide
and does not provide dosing or protocols. Evidence ranges from approved clinical use in some
countries to purely preclinical signals. Consult a qualified clinician before making any
decision.
:::

Inflammation and immune modulation spans tissue inflammation, immune support, and related
processes. Evidence quality varies widely here: one entry is clinically used for specific
indications in some jurisdictions, while others rest on animal data. The links below show the
strongest applicable evidence tier and note regulatory status.

## Relevant peptides

- [Thymosin Alpha-1](../peptides/thymosin-alpha-1.md) (Clinical, Preclinical): immune modulator approved in some countries for specific uses.
- [BPC-157](../peptides/bpc-157.md) (Preclinical, Mechanistic): animal models of GI and tissue inflammation. Not approved.
- [TB-500](../peptides/tb-500.md) (Preclinical, Mechanistic): animal data on inflammatory and repair responses. Not approved.
- [GHK-Cu](../peptides/ghk-cu.md) (Preclinical, Mechanistic): tissue-remodeling and antioxidant-response research.

See [how evidence tiers work](../how-it-works.md) to interpret these labels.
