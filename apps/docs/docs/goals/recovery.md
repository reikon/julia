# Peptides for recovery

:::warning Educational use only
This page is informational and is **not medical advice**. It does not recommend any peptide
and does not provide dosing or protocols. Most peptides discussed for recovery have only
preclinical or anecdotal support. Consult a qualified clinician before making any decision.
:::

Recovery covers tissue repair after injury or training, including tendon, ligament, muscle,
and connective tissue. This is one of the most heavily marketed peptide categories, and it is
also one where the human evidence is weakest. The links below show the strongest tier of
evidence that currently applies to each peptide for this goal.

## Relevant peptides

- [BPC-157](../peptides/bpc-157.md) (Preclinical, Anecdotal): rodent tissue-repair studies, no robust human trials.
- [TB-500](../peptides/tb-500.md) (Preclinical, Anecdotal): Thymosin Beta-4-related; animal healing data.
- [GHK-Cu](../peptides/ghk-cu.md) (Preclinical, Mechanistic): tissue remodeling and wound-healing research.
- [CJC-1295](../peptides/cjc-1295.md) (Anecdotal, Mechanistic): GHRH analog; recovery claims are self-reported.
- [Ipamorelin](../peptides/ipamorelin.md) (Anecdotal, Mechanistic): GH secretagogue; recovery claims are self-reported.
- [Sermorelin](../peptides/sermorelin.md) (Mechanistic, Anecdotal): GHRH analog with a GH-axis clinical history.

See [how evidence tiers work](../how-it-works.md) to interpret these labels.
