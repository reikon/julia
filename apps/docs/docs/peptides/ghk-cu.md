# GHK-Cu

:::warning Educational use only
This article is informational and is **not medical advice**. It does not provide dosing,
protocols, or instructions for human use. Topical cosmetic use of GHK-Cu differs from any
injectable or systemic use. Consult a qualified clinician before making any health decision.
:::

## Overview

GHK-Cu is a naturally occurring copper-binding tripeptide (glycyl-L-histidyl-L-lysine bound
to a copper ion), also known as copper tripeptide-1. Its level in human plasma declines with
age. GHK-Cu is best known as a cosmetic skincare ingredient, where topical formulations have
been studied for skin appearance, and it is also studied preclinically for wound healing and
tissue remodeling.

Research status at a glance: some **clinical** and applied research exists for topical
cosmetic skin endpoints; broader regenerative and longevity claims are largely
**preclinical** and **mechanistic**, with **anecdotal** community use.

## Mechanism (Mechanistic)

GHK-Cu is proposed to modulate copper homeostasis and to influence gene expression related
to tissue remodeling, antioxidant response, and extracellular matrix synthesis (for example
collagen). It is studied as a signaling and copper-delivery peptide. These are mechanistic
and preclinical findings outside of the topical cosmetic context.

## Safety and considerations

Topical cosmetic GHK-Cu is generally regarded as well tolerated in formulations, though
individual reactions can occur. Evidence for systemic or injectable use is limited, and
copper balance is a consideration. Regulatory status differs between cosmetic ingredients and
any therapeutic use. Informational only, not a recommendation to use.
