# Ipamorelin

## Overview

Ipamorelin is a synthetic pentapeptide classed as a growth hormone secretagogue. It acts as
a selective agonist at the ghrelin receptor (the growth hormone secretagogue receptor) and is
studied for its ability to stimulate growth hormone release with relatively limited effect on
other hormones such as cortisol or prolactin. It is widely discussed in recovery and
body-composition communities but lacks large controlled human trials for these uses.

Research status at a glance: largely **preclinical** and early pharmacology, with a defined
**mechanistic** basis and extensive **anecdotal** use.

## Mechanism (Mechanistic)

Ipamorelin mimics ghrelin at the growth hormone secretagogue receptor, prompting the
pituitary to release growth hormone in a relatively selective manner. This is a mechanistic
and pharmacological rationale rather than a body of human outcome evidence.

## Safety and considerations

Human safety data is limited. As with other GH secretagogues, effects on the GH and IGF-1
axis are a consideration, and gray-market purity is a concern. Regulatory status varies and it
is not approved for human therapeutic use in major markets. Informational only, not a
recommendation to use.
