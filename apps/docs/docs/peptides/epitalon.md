## Overview

Epitalon (also spelled epithalon) is a synthetic tetrapeptide (sequence Ala-Glu-Asp-Gly)
developed as a synthetic version of epithalamin, a pineal-gland peptide. It is studied,
largely in Russian research literature, in the context of aging and the pineal-melatonin
system. The popular longevity and telomere-related claims around it rest mainly on
preclinical work and have not been confirmed by large independent human trials.

Research status at a glance: mainly **preclinical** with some early human-research claims that
warrant independent confirmation, plus **mechanistic** rationale and **anecdotal** use.

## Mechanism (Mechanistic)

Epitalon is proposed to influence the pineal gland and melatonin regulation and has been
discussed in connection with telomerase activity in preclinical settings. These mechanisms are
proposed and primarily preclinical; the strength and reproducibility of the underlying
evidence is an open question.

## Safety and considerations

Human safety data is limited and the independent evidence base is thin. Gray-market purity is
a concern, and broad longevity claims should be treated with caution given the limited
independent replication. Regulatory status varies and it is not approved for human therapeutic
use in major markets. Informational only.
