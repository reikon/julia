## Overview

CJC-1295 is a synthetic analog of growth hormone-releasing hormone (GHRH). It is designed to
stimulate the body's own release of growth hormone. A variant includes a drug affinity
complex (DAC) intended to extend its duration of action. It is widely discussed in
body-composition and longevity communities but lacks the large controlled human trials needed
to establish efficacy or safety for these popular uses.

Research status at a glance: largely **preclinical** and early pharmacology, with a defined
**mechanistic** rationale and extensive **anecdotal** use.

## Mechanism (Mechanistic)

As a GHRH analog, CJC-1295 is proposed to bind GHRH receptors in the pituitary and increase
the pulsatile release of endogenous growth hormone and, downstream, IGF-1. This is a
mechanistic rationale supported by pharmacology studies rather than by large human outcome
trials.

## Safety and considerations

Human safety data is limited. Effects on the GH and IGF-1 axis are a consideration, and
gray-market purity is a concern. Regulatory status varies and it is not approved for human
therapeutic use in major markets. Often discussed alongside ipamorelin in community settings;
this article does not endorse or describe any combination or protocol. Informational only.
