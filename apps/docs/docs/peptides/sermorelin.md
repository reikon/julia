## Overview

Sermorelin is a synthetic peptide corresponding to the first 29 amino acids of growth
hormone-releasing hormone (GHRH 1-29), the active fragment of GHRH. It has a notable clinical
history: it was previously studied and used as a diagnostic and therapeutic agent for growth
hormone evaluation, including in pediatric growth contexts, though some branded products have
since been discontinued. It is now also discussed in longevity and anti-aging settings.

Research status at a glance: a real **clinical** history exists for GH-axis diagnostic and
therapeutic use; many popular longevity claims rest on **mechanistic** rationale and
**anecdotal** reports.

## Mechanism (Clinical and mechanistic)

As a GHRH analog, sermorelin binds pituitary GHRH receptors and stimulates the body's own
release of growth hormone. This mechanism is established and was the basis for its diagnostic
use in evaluating GH secretion.

## Safety and considerations

Sermorelin has a documented clinical history with a characterized safety context for its
studied uses, though availability and regulatory status have changed and vary by region.
Effects on the GH and IGF-1 axis are a consideration. This article does not provide dosing.
Refer to current regulatory information and a licensed clinician.
