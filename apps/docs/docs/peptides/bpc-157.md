## Overview

BPC-157 (Body Protection Compound-157) is a synthetic pentadecapeptide: a chain of 15 amino
acids whose sequence is derived from a protein reported to occur in human gastric juice. It
is studied mainly in animal models for tissue repair, and it appears in the literature under
names such as PL 14736. BPC-157 has been the subject of preclinical research for several
decades, but it has not progressed through the large, controlled human trials that would be
needed to establish efficacy or safety for human use.

Research status at a glance: predominantly **preclinical** (rodent studies), with proposed
**mechanistic** explanations and a large volume of **anecdotal** community reports. There is
no robust human clinical trial evidence base for the popular recovery uses discussed online.

## Mechanism (Mechanistic)

The proposed mechanism, drawn from preclinical work, is that BPC-157 supports tissue repair
by promoting angiogenesis (the formation of new blood vessels) and by modulating
growth-factor signaling and nitric oxide pathways involved in healing. These are proposed
and partially studied mechanisms in animal and in-vitro systems. They should be read as
theory and preclinical signal, not as demonstrated human outcomes.

## Safety and considerations

Human safety data is limited because there is little controlled human research. Reported
risks and unknowns come largely from animal studies and community reports, which are not a
substitute for clinical safety data. Purity and identity of gray-market material are a
recognized concern. Regulatory status varies by jurisdiction, and BPC-157 is not approved for
human therapeutic use in major markets. This section is informational and is not guidance to
use the compound.
