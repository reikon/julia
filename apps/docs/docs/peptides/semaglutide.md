## Overview

Semaglutide is a glucagon-like peptide-1 (GLP-1) receptor agonist. Unlike most peptides in
this reference, it is an **approved prescription drug**, marketed under brand names including
Ozempic and Rybelsus for type 2 diabetes and Wegovy for chronic weight management, with
approvals from regulators such as the US FDA and the European Medicines Agency. It is one of
the most heavily studied peptides in this reference and has a substantial human clinical
evidence base.

Research status at a glance: strong **clinical** evidence for its approved indications, a
well-characterized **mechanism**, and broad **anecdotal** discussion online.

## Mechanism (Clinical and mechanistic)

Semaglutide mimics the incretin hormone GLP-1. By activating GLP-1 receptors it increases
glucose-dependent insulin secretion, reduces glucagon, slows gastric emptying, and acts on
appetite-regulating regions of the brain to reduce hunger. These effects are documented in
human clinical research.
