# Semax

:::warning Educational use only
This article is informational and is **not medical advice**. It does not provide dosing,

the United States or the European Union. Consult a qualified clinician before making any

:::

## Overview

Semax is a synthetic peptide derived from a fragment of adrenocorticotropic hormone (an
ACTH 4-10 analog), developed in Russia and studied there for cognitive and neuroprotective
applications, including in stroke recovery settings within Russian clinical practice. Most of
the literature is Russian-sourced, and it is not approved in Western markets, where the
independent evidence base is limited.

Research status at a glance: mainly **preclinical** with Russian-sourced clinical claims,
plus **mechanistic** rationale and **anecdotal** nootropic use.

## Mechanism (Mechanistic)

Semax is proposed to influence brain-derived neurotrophic factor (BDNF) and related
neurotrophic signaling, as well as neurotransmitter and neuroprotective pathways. These
mechanisms are drawn largely from preclinical research and the Russian clinical literature.

## Safety and considerations

Independent human safety data is limited, and much evidence comes from one national research
tradition not broadly replicated. Gray-market purity is a concern. Regulatory status: not
approved for human use in major Western markets. Informational only.
