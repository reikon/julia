## Overview

PT-141, known by the generic name bremelanotide, is a melanocortin receptor agonist. It is an
**approved prescription drug** (marketed as Vyleesi) for hypoactive sexual desire disorder in
certain premenopausal women, with approval from the US FDA. It is derived from research on
melanocortin signaling and is also discussed more broadly online for libido.

Research status at a glance: **clinical** evidence supports its specific approved indication;
broader off-label and community uses rely on **mechanistic** rationale and **anecdotal**
reports.

## Mechanism (Clinical and mechanistic)

Bremelanotide activates melanocortin receptors in the central nervous system, a pathway
implicated in sexual desire and arousal. Unlike vasodilator drugs, it acts centrally rather
than primarily on blood flow. This central melanocortin mechanism is the basis for its
studied use.

## Safety and considerations

As an approved drug, bremelanotide has documented labeling, common side effects such as
nausea and transient blood pressure changes, and specific warnings and contraindications. Use
outside the approved indication or via gray-market sources adds risk. This article does not
provide dosing. Refer to official prescribing information and a licensed clinician.
