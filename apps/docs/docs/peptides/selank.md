# Selank

:::warning Educational use only
This article is informational and is **not medical advice**. It does not provide dosing,
protocols, or instructions for human use. Selank is **not an approved drug for human use** in
the United States or the European Union. Consult a qualified clinician before making any
health decision.
:::

## Overview

Selank is a synthetic peptide derived from the immune-related peptide tuftsin, developed in
Russia and studied there as an anxiolytic (anti-anxiety) agent. Most of the research
literature is from Russian sources, and it is sometimes described as used clinically in that
context. In Western markets it is not approved, and the independent evidence base is limited.

Research status at a glance: mainly **preclinical** with Russian-sourced research claims,
plus **mechanistic** rationale and **anecdotal** use for mood and focus.

## Mechanism (Mechanistic)

Selank is proposed to influence neurotransmitter systems and to affect the expression of
brain-derived neurotrophic factor (BDNF) and the GABA and serotonin systems, as well as
certain immune-related pathways. These are proposed mechanisms drawn largely from preclinical
work.

## Safety and considerations

Independent human safety data is limited, and much of the evidence comes from a single
national research tradition that has not been broadly replicated. Gray-market purity is a
concern. Regulatory status: not approved for human use in major Western markets.
Informational only.
