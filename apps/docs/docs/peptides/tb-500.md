# TB-500

:::warning Educational use only
This article is informational and is **not medical advice**. It does not provide dosing,
protocols, or instructions for human use. TB-500 is **not an approved drug for human use**.
Consult a qualified clinician before making any health decision.
:::

## Overview

TB-500 is a synthetic peptide related to Thymosin Beta-4 (Tbeta4), a naturally occurring
protein involved in cell migration and tissue repair. TB-500 is commonly described as a
fragment or analog representing an active region of Thymosin Beta-4. It is studied in animal
models for healing and is widely discussed in sports and recovery communities, but it lacks
the controlled human trials needed to establish efficacy or safety for human use.

Research status at a glance: mainly **preclinical**, with **mechanistic** rationale and a
substantial **anecdotal** literature. It is also referenced on anti-doping prohibited lists.

## Mechanism (Mechanistic)

Thymosin Beta-4 binds and sequesters actin, influencing cell movement, and is proposed to
support angiogenesis, cell migration, and tissue regeneration. TB-500 is proposed to share
these activities. These are mechanistic and preclinical observations, not demonstrated human
clinical outcomes.

## Safety and considerations

Human safety data is limited. Most information comes from animal studies and self-reports,
which do not establish a human safety profile. Gray-market purity is a concern, and TB-500 is
prohibited in competitive sport. Regulatory status varies and it is not approved for human
therapeutic use in major markets. Informational only.
