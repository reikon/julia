# Thymosin Alpha-1

## Overview

Thymosin Alpha-1 is a peptide originally isolated from the thymus and known generically as
thymalfasin (brand name Zadaxin in some regions). It is studied as an immune modulator. Its
regulatory status differs sharply by country: it is approved and used clinically for certain
indications (for example some viral hepatitis settings and as an adjuvant) in a number of
countries, while it is not approved for those uses in others, including the United States.

Research status at a glance: meaningful **clinical** research and approvals exist in some
jurisdictions, supported by **preclinical** and **mechanistic** work, with **anecdotal**
community use for general immune support.

## Mechanism (Clinical and mechanistic)

Thymosin Alpha-1 is proposed to modulate immune function, including effects on T-cell
maturation and function and on inflammatory and antiviral responses. These immune-modulating
mechanisms underpin its studied clinical uses.

## Safety and considerations

In jurisdictions where it is approved, Thymosin Alpha-1 has a clinical safety context for its
studied indications. Outside those uses, evidence is more limited and regulatory status
varies, so general immune-boosting claims should be read cautiously. This article does not
provide dosing. Refer to regional regulatory information and a licensed clinician.
