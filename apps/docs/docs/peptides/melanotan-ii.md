## Overview

Melanotan II is a synthetic analog of alpha-melanocyte-stimulating hormone (alpha-MSH), a
melanocortin agonist. It is widely sold on the gray market and discussed for skin tanning and
related effects. It is **not approved for human use**, and multiple regulators have warned
consumers about unlicensed melanotan products and associated risks.

Research status at a glance: largely **preclinical** and early pharmacology, with a defined
**mechanistic** rationale and substantial **anecdotal** use accompanied by safety warnings.

## Mechanism (Mechanistic)

As a non-selective melanocortin receptor agonist, Melanotan II is proposed to stimulate
melanogenesis (melanin production) and to influence appetite and sexual-function pathways via
melanocortin signaling. These are mechanistic and preclinical observations.

## Safety and considerations

Safety concerns are significant. Regulators have warned about unlicensed melanotan products,
reported side effects, changes in moles and pigmentation, and the risks of unregulated
sources and self-administration. There is no established human safety profile for approved
use. This section is informational and is not a recommendation to use; it underscores
documented risks.
