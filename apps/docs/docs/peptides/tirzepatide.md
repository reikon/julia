## Overview

Tirzepatide is a dual agonist that activates both the GIP (glucose-dependent insulinotropic
polypeptide) and GLP-1 receptors. Like semaglutide, it is an **approved prescription drug**,
marketed as Mounjaro for type 2 diabetes and Zepbound for chronic weight management, with
approvals from regulators such as the US FDA. It has a substantial and growing human clinical
evidence base.

Research status at a glance: strong **clinical** evidence for approved indications, a defined
dual-incretin **mechanism**, and wide **anecdotal** discussion.

## Mechanism (Clinical and mechanistic)

By activating both GIP and GLP-1 receptors, tirzepatide enhances glucose-dependent insulin
secretion, affects glucagon, slows gastric emptying, and reduces appetite. The dual-incretin
approach is the basis for its study in metabolic disease, supported by human trials.
