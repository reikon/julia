## Overview

MOTS-c (mitochondrial open reading frame of the twelve S rRNA type-c) is a mitochondrial-
derived peptide, a small peptide encoded within mitochondrial DNA. It is a relatively recent
research subject studied for metabolism, exercise, and aging biology. Interest is high in
longevity circles, but the work is largely preclinical and mechanistic, with little
controlled human outcome data.

Research status at a glance: mainly **preclinical** and **mechanistic**, with growing research
interest and emerging **anecdotal** use.

## Mechanism (Mechanistic)

MOTS-c is proposed to act on metabolic regulation, including pathways such as AMPK signaling,
and to influence insulin sensitivity, metabolic homeostasis, and cellular stress responses. It
is studied as a signal linking mitochondrial function to whole-body metabolism. These are
mechanistic and preclinical findings.

## Safety and considerations

Human safety data is essentially absent given how new the research is. Gray-market purity is a
concern, and claims should be treated as early-stage. Regulatory status: not approved for
human therapeutic use. Informational only, not a recommendation to use.
