Lean Startup MVP Recommendation

Do NOT start with:

prescriptions
direct peptide sales
shipping
inventory
fulfillment

That creates massive complexity immediately.

Start with:

Research database
Search/discovery engine
Provider directory
Verification system
Educational content

That is enough to validate:

demand
SEO traction
user behavior
provider interest
trust positioning

before expanding.

SWOT Analysis
Strengths
Massive information gap in a rapidly growing industry.
Strong SEO/content flywheel potential.
High trust moat if verification is done properly.
Network effects between consumers, providers, and vendors.
Weaknesses
Heavy compliance and legal sensitivity.
Requires scientific credibility to gain trust.
High effort needed for accurate data curation and moderation.
Potential misinformation/reputation risks.
Opportunities
Explosive growth in longevity and preventative medicine.
AI can dramatically accelerate research summarization.
Becoming the “default search engine” for peptide intelligence.
Expansion into broader longevity therapeutics and personalized medicine.
Threats
FDA/regulatory crackdowns on peptide-related businesses.
Legal exposure from medical claims.
Competitors copying surface-level directory features.
Reputation damage if low-quality vendors/providers slip through verification.

The most important strategic insight here is this:

The marketplace is not the moat.
Trust is the moat.
Structured data is the moat.
Verification is the moat.
Network effects are the moat.

That’s the difference between building:

a temporary affiliate website

versus

a category-defining healthcare intelligence company.
